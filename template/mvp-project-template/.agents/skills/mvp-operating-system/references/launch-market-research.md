# Launch Market Research

Use Launch Market Research as the second mandatory step for every new MVP or project restart, after Idea Intake.

## Purpose

Assess whether the MVP can be realistically launched through available channels, network, minimal budget, partnerships, and visible competitor demand.

Do not produce TAM/SAM/SOM analysis. Focus on practical launch evidence.

## Required fields

```text
Competitors:
<direct competitors and close substitutes>

Alternatives:
<how users solve the problem without direct competitors>

Pricing:
<prices, packages, free tiers, trials, commissions, or terms competitors use>

User complaints:
<what users criticize in competitors or alternatives>

User praise:
<what users value in competitors or alternatives>

Acquisition channels:
<channels competitors appear to use>

Visible ads:
<where competitor ads, sponsored posts, SEO, listings, or creator mentions are visible>

Partner/referral mechanics:
<affiliate, referral, reseller, partnership, or community mechanics competitors use>

Launch opportunities:
<what can be reached through network, minimal budget, partnerships, communities, or manual outreach>

Launch risks:
<risks visible before launch: weak demand, expensive traffic, trust barriers, regulation, crowded channels, low willingness to pay>

Recommendation:
<launch / narrow / reposition / delay, with a short reason>
```

## Rules

- Prefer recent and source-backed evidence when external information is needed.
- Keep the output compact and decision-oriented.
- Do not ask the human for data that can be researched by agents.
- Escalate only if the launch path changes MVP scope, market, pricing, risk, or budget.
- If web access is unavailable, create a best-effort research plan and mark evidence as missing.
